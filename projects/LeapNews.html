Overview

As part of my journey toward a career in UX design, I completed the Google UX Design Certificate, a seven-course program on Coursera. This intensive training provided a solid foundation in UX principles and practical skills, culminating in a series of projects, including the Leap News App. Designed to deliver a trustworthy and seamless news experience, Leap News targets well-educated readers seeking the latest information from reliable sources. My goal was to create an intuitive, user-friendly interface that enables users to browse news and articles effortlessly.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Competitor Analysis

In conducting a competitor analysis for Leap News, I reviewed key features offered by prominent news apps such as The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, BBC News, and The News Lens. The analysis focused on essential functionalities like article recommendations, multilingual accessibility, personalized themes, video content, save-for-later options, and the ability to follow authors. While each app excels in specific areas, gaps remain, particularly in providing a well-rounded, personalized experience. Leap News aims to address these gaps by integrating comprehensive, user-focused features that cater to a global, well-educated audience looking for a good digital reading experience.
