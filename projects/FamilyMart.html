Overview

Taiwan's second-largest convenience store chain, FamilyMart, launched its app in 2016, and it has since been downloaded over 6 million times. The app offers features like mobile payment, online shopping, coffee pre-orders, and digital coupons. Despite its popularity, the app has received relatively low ratings, with a 3.4/5 on Google Play and 2.7/5 on the App Store. Because of this, I decided to take on a side project to redesign the FamilyMart app.

Project Scope

FamilyMart app develops consumer loyalty programs and integrate online and offline consumption scenarios for standing out in a highly competitive market. Its vision of seamless shopping experience allows customers to browse featured products and new launches and easily order right away. Two main redesign missions are selected:

(1) Ordering Process
(2) Purchased Items Transferring Process

Usability Test

Tasks

In the user research phase, I conducted usability test with 4 participants and assigned them three tasks to evaluate the FamilyMart app. The tasks were:
(1) Locating the feature of gifting products to a friend.
(2) Ordering Process: Adding Wandan Milk to the cart and completing an online payment
(3) Gifting Process: Transferring an item to a friend.

Key Findings

Feature Accessibility Issues
- Participants faced challenges locating the "Gifting to a Friend" feature on the homepage.
- The feature was not visually distinct, with unclear button placement and color, causing delays and confusion.

Feature Confusion Between Similar Pages
- Users reported difficulty distinguishing between the "Redeem my Order" page and the "Gifting to a Friend" page.
- Despite being functionally different, the pages shared similar layouts and design elements, leading to user uncertainty about their purpose.

Complex Pricing Information
- Participants expressed frustration with the pricing information displayed during the ordering and gifting processes.
- The interface presented excessive numerical details, making it hard for users to identify key pricing elements such as total cost or discounts.

Design Problems

Design Challenges

(1) Diverse Digital Services:
Taiwan's convenience stores offer highly versatile digital services. For example, customers can use the app to pre-purchase multiple coffees through the "Buy Now, Pick Up at Any Store" service, which allows them to pick up their orders across different store locations and on different days. Another service, "Buy Now, Pickup Later" lets users pre-order items online and collect them from a designated store near their home.

(2) Complex Pricing and Purchase Strategies:
Taiwan's convenience store culture includes unique promotions, such as discounts for bulk coffee purchases. However, chains often limit options to fixed quantities (e.g., 15, 25, or 50 cups) that must be paid for upfront but can be picked up in batches. Additionally, pricing strategies vary significantly across product types. For instance, green tea may require a minimum purchase of six bottles at a set price, whereas soda is typically priced per bottle. The inconsistent pricing and quantity requirements create challenges in designing user-friendly and flexible systems.

(3) Gifting Services:
Taiwan's convenience stores also provide a service that allows customers to purchase coffee or beverages and send them as gifts to others. This adds an extra layer of complexity to the system, as it requires seamless integration of product selection, recipient details, and notifications while maintaining an intuitive user experience.
